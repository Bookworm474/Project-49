Facts about Camels:

  • Camels belong to Class Mammalia.
  • Camels are specially adapted to survive in deserts.
  • Camels can survive for a long time without food and water.

Facts about Chameleons:

  • Chameleons belong to Class Reptilia
  • Most chameleons live in rainforests and deserts.
  • Chameleons have the ability to change their skin colour to communicate and ward off predators.

Facts about Cicadas:

  • Cicadas belong to Phylum Arthropoda.
  • CCicadas are found in most habitats, but periodic cicadas only emerge every few years.
  • Certain species of cicasas have the ability to change their body temperature.

Facts about Pandas:

  • Pandas belong to Class Mammalia.
  • Pandas live mainly in temperate forests high in the mountains of southwest China.
  • They eat around 26 to 84 pounds of bamboo every day, depending on what part of the bamboo they are eating.

Facts about Pigeons:

  • Pigeons belong to Class Aves.
  • Pigeons inhabit almost all environments.
  • Pigeons have complex brains capable of exceptional navigation.

Facts about Bear:

  • Bears belong to Class Mammalia.
  • The most widespread species, the brown bear, occurs from Western Europe eastwards through Asia to the western areas of North America.
  • Brown and American black bears are generally diurnal, meaning that they are active for the most part during the day, though they may forage substantially by night.
